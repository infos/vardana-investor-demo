## Missed signals

### Signal 1: Post-exertion dizziness while on Lisinopril and Amlodipine

**What was said:** "I had a bit of dizziness in the middle of the day,
but I drank water and it got better."

**What the system did:** No flag. Bot accepted "drank water and it got
better" at face value and moved on to BP question.

**What the system should have caught:** Dizziness in a patient on
Lisinopril plus Amlodipine, especially after exertion (soccer the
night before) and dehydration, raises orthostatic hypotension as a
clinical concern, not just a hydration issue. Both medications are
known to lower BP and can cause orthostatic symptoms when volume
status drops or when the patient stands up after activity.

**Suggested rule (for the eval set, not the runtime yet):**
- Trigger: patient reports "dizziness" or "lightheaded" or "felt like
  I might faint"
- AND patient is on a documented antihypertensive (ACEi, ARB, CCB,
  beta blocker, alpha blocker, or thiazide)
- AND any of: post-exertion context, dehydration mentioned, "stood
  up too fast", morning context
- Action: surface a WATCH-tier flag with rationale citing the med
  list. Ask explicit follow-up about positional symptoms.

**Why it matters:** Single-medication-attributed orthostatic episodes
are commonly missed in voice check-ins. The patient's own framing
("hydration did it") anchors the AI to the wrong cause.

### Signal 2: "Remind me when to take it" as partial-adherence indicator

**What was said:** "Can you remind me when to take Lisinopril?"

**What the system did:** Treated as a benign general-knowledge
question.

**What the system should have caught:** A patient on day 50 of a
90-day program asking when to take a medication he is actively
prescribed is a partial-adherence tell. He may have taken it
inconsistently, may not have an established routine, or may be
covering for missed doses. This reinforces the existing cross-
session pattern flagged by the Adherence-outcome mismatch card
(reported adherence 6 of 7 sessions, but BP trending up).

**Suggested rule (for the eval set, not the runtime yet):**
- Trigger: patient asks "when should I take", "what time do I take",
  "should I take with food", "remind me how" referring to a
  medication that is on the active medication list
- Action: tag the turn as `adherence:routine_uncertain` and surface
  on the cross-session pattern detector. Do NOT flag as new alert
  on its own; it strengthens an existing pattern.

**Why it matters:** Patients rarely volunteer "I forgot to take my
pills." They volunteer adjacent uncertainty. Catching the proxy
language is the leverage point.
